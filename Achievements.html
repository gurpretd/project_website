INDEE is the ongoing Indo-Norwegian project approved by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Government of Norway through SINTEF and NTNU, Norway. Main purpose of INDEE project is to eliminate the use of synthetic refrigerants from developing countries. An “International Workshop on Integrated CO2 Refrigeration Systems for LT, MT & HT Applications and Supermarkets” as an important part of INDEE project to disseminate CO2 technology was organized on 12th-13th March 2018 at Indian Institute of Technology Madras.The workshop appeared as a positive networking event and as a knowledge sharing platform with attendance from various government, non-government organizations, research scholars and professional bodies.

Dr. Krzysztof Banasiak, Research Scientist, SINTEF Energy Systems projected INDEE progress and various achievements for Indian supermarket during the workshop. The various possible options available in the CO2 test facility (INDEE) located at IIT Madras are also discussed by Dr. Banasiak. The potential of CO2 system as a possible source of heat recovery at high ambient temperature and the future perspective of the technology was projected. The positive response from the researchers and other government officials present in the workshop ,anticipated the importance of this technology in the nearer future.
